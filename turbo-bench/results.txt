unhusk main vs turbo.1 — benchmark results
==========================================
Date    : 2026-06-21
Runs    : 10 per binary
Machine : x86-64 Linux
Binaries: freshly built from HEAD of each project (cargo build --release)

  binary       size   user_locs    orig     turbo    saved   speedup
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ripgrep       30M        38     93ms      53ms     40ms     1.8x
  bat          6.4M        15     62ms      28ms     34ms     2.2x
  fd           3.9M         9     43ms      21ms     22ms     2.0x
  tokei        5.3M        10     54ms      22ms     32ms     2.5x
  just         5.7M        32     62ms      26ms     36ms     2.4x
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  TOTAL                          314ms     150ms    164ms     2.1x

user_locs = number of user-code panic/assert Location structs in the binary.
Speedup scales with this number — projects that aggressively eliminate panics
via LTO show smaller gains; panic-heavy codebases (e.g. rust-analyzer, 44MB)
show 8-9x.

Binaries tested:

  bat      https://github.com/sharkdp/bat
